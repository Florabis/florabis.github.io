# **CONSTITUTION OF THE STATE OF FLORABÍS**

(Constitución del Estado Real de Florabís)

Ratified on 08/25/2025

---

## **PREAMBLE**

We, the People of the State of Florabís, do hereby ordain and establish this Constitution for the government of our State, in union with the Imperial Federation.

Formerly known as Pristina, our land has been renewed and reconstituted as El Estado Real de Florabís, a Semi-Constitutional Council Monarchy guided by its Soberante and administered through its Royal Council.

Florabís shall be a welcoming State, open to newfriends and veterans alike, where every citizen is guaranteed protection, opportunity, and the ability to contribute to the prosperity of the community. United by tradition, strengthened by service, and governed by law, Florabís shall endure and flourish as both a home and a beacon within the Imperial Federation.

---

## **ARTICLE I – THE STATE**

§1. The State shall be known federally as the State of Florabís, and internally as El Estado Real de Florabís.

§2. Florabís is a constituent state of the Imperial Federation, possessing autonomy in its internal affairs subject to the Federal Constitution.

§3. Florabís shall be organized as a Semi-Constitutional Council Monarchy (Monarquía Consejista Semi-Constitucional), vested in the Soberante and administered through the Royal Council.

§4. The capital of Florabís shall be San Alejandro de Guadalupe.

§5. The official languages of Florabís shall be English and Spanish.

§6. The people of Florabís shall be known as Florabinos.

§7. The official motto of Florabís shall be: “Lo que no me mata me hará más fuerte.”

---

## **ARTICLE II – REPRESENTATION IN THE IF SENATE**

§1. Florabís shall be represented in the Senate of the Imperial Federation according to its population, as provided by the Federal Constitution.

§2. The first Senate seat, if granted, shall be held by the Soberante, unless the Soberante chooses to delegate it. In such a case, the Soberante shall appoint another citizen of Florabís to serve as Senator, subject to confirmation by the Royal Council.

§2a. The Soberante shall be recognized as the State Leader of Florabís within the Imperial Federation, entrusted with representing the State in all federal matters.

§3. The second Senate seat, if granted, shall be appointed by the Soberante.

§4. Any additional Senate seats, if granted, shall be filled from among the members of the Royal Council, nominated by majority vote of the Council and confirmed by the Soberante.

§5. Senators shall act as the lawful representatives of Florabís and shall defend the interests of the State within the Imperial Federation.

---

## **ARTICLE III – RIGHTS AND DUTIES OF CITIZENS**

§1. All citizens shall enjoy the right to freedom of speech, religion, and association.

§2. All citizens shall enjoy the right to due process of law and to a fair trial.

§3. All citizens shall enjoy the right to own, use, and enjoy property.

§4. All citizens shall enjoy the right to participate in the governance of the State as provided in this Constitution.

§5. All citizens shall enjoy the right to farm and cultivate land, as farmland is one of the foundations of the State’s prosperity.

§6. Citizens shall obey the laws of Florabís and of the Imperial Federation.

§7. Citizens shall respect their fellow citizens and the institutions of the State.

§8. Citizens shall contribute to the prosperity and defense of Florabís through labor, farming, mining, or service.

---

## **ARTICLE IV – CITIZENSHIP TIERS**

§1. Citizenship shall be organized into three tiers:

* Tier I – Vecino de Florabís (Neighbor)

  Newly admitted citizens shall be entitled to protection, basic housing, and farmland for survival.

    * May not vote, hold office, or access mining zones.

    * Required to contribute basic labor toward settlement upkeep.

* Tier II – Ciudadano Real (Royal Citizen)

  Recognized citizens, entitled to:

    * Full property rights within Florabís.

    * The right to acquire and hold farmland, upon purchase or grant.

    * The right to vote in state referenda.

    * Access to designated mining zones, as established by the Soberante and the Royal Council.

    * Eligibility to seek advancement to Tier III upon payment of a one-time fee to the State Treasury, set in diamonds (d).

    * Eligibility to run for election as Magistrado del Pueblo.

* And required to:

    * Support and participate in public works and projects.

* Tier III – Hidalgo del Estado (Esteemed Citizen)

  Citizens of highest loyalty, entitled to:

    * All rights of Tier II.

    * Access to all available open mines of Florabís.

    * Eligibility for appointment to the Royal Council.

    * Eligibility to serve in the State Judiciary where permitted by this Constitution, including eligibility to run for Magistrado del Pueblo.

    * Recognition for exceptional service and loyalty.

* And required to:

    * Own and maintain farmland within Florabís, as agriculture is one of the foundations of the State’s prosperity.

    * Provide counsel and leadership within the community.

§2. Advancement between tiers shall be based on loyalty, contribution, and the fulfillment of requirements set for each tier, as determined by the Royal Council with the assent of the Soberante.

§3. Demotion shall be permitted for inactivity, disloyalty, or neglect of duties, upon enactment by the Royal Council with the assent of the Soberante.

§4. Applications for citizenship shall be reviewed by the Royal Council, subject to final approval by the Soberante.

§5. Citizenship may be revoked only by decree of the Soberante, with recommendations from the Royal Council.

§6. Citizens shall follow Common Procedures.

---

## **ARTICLE V – THE SOBERANTE**

§1. The Soberante shall be Head of State and Head of Government, presiding over all institutions of Florabís and serving as preserver of its traditions and unity.

§2. The Soberante shall:

* Preside, over the Royal Council as Presidente del Consejo.

* Hold the power of veto over acts and decisions of the Royal Council.

* Issue Royal Decrees having the force of law.

§3. The Soberante shall:

* Appoint the Alcalde Mayor and other Councilors of the Royal Council.

* Appoint the Capitán de la Guardia, who shall thereby also serve as Councilor for Defense and Security.

* Serve as Commander-in-Chief of the Florabís Defense Guard.

* Exercise control over the Treasury, assets, namelayers, and official Discord communications.

§4. The Soberante may entrust specific powers or responsibilities to the Alcalde Mayor or to Councilors, while retaining supreme authority over all acts of government.

§5. The Capitán de la Guardia is the third in command of the State, under the authority of the Soberante and the Alcalde Mayor.

§6. The Soberante shall remain in office without term, unless voluntarily abdicated or succeeded in accordance with law.

§7. In the event of war, invasion, or grave danger to Florabís, the Soberante may declare a State of Emergency. During such a period, the Soberante may issue decrees necessary for the defense and survival of the State. A State of Emergency shall expire after two weeks unless renewed with the assent of the Royal Council.

---

## **ARTICLE VI – THE ALCALDE MAYOR**

§1. The Alcalde Mayor shall be the Senior Officer of the State, acting under the authority of the Soberante.

§2. The Alcalde Mayor shall be appointed by the Soberante at the commencement of each Royal Council term, from among its members.

§3. The Alcalde Mayor shall serve for the duration of that Council term, which shall coincide with the federal election cycle of the Imperial Federation, unless reappointed or lawfully removed.

§4. The Alcalde Mayor shall:

* Coordinate the work of the Royal Council.

* Oversee daily administration.

* Execute decrees and directives of the Soberante.

* Assist in the management of the Treasury, farmland, and public works.

---

## **ARTICLE VII – THE ROYAL COUNCIL (CONSEJO REAL)**

§1. The Royal Council shall serve as both the principal executive body and the legislative assembly of Florabís.

§1a. The Magistrado Consejero shall retain their seat and vote on the Royal Council while serving as Magistrate, but shall be prohibited from presiding over or rendering judgment in cases directly involving the Royal Council or in cases where the Magistrado Consejero is personally a party.

§2. The Royal Council shall consist of:

* The Alcalde Mayor.

* Councilors appointed by the Soberante.

§3. Each Councilor shall be assigned a domain, which may include:

* Defense and Security

* Works and Infrastructure

* Economy and Finance

* Justice and Law

* Culture and Heritage

* Agriculture and Resources

* Mining and Natural Resources

* Population and Settlement

§4. Appointments to the Royal Council shall normally coincide with the federal election cycle of the Imperial Federation. Councilors shall serve until the next federal election, at which time the Soberante may either renew their appointment or appoint a successor.

§5. At the conclusion of their term, any Councilor may petition for renewal of their appointment. The Soberante shall give due consideration to such petitions. A reappointed Councilor may, if they wish, be assigned to a different domain than their previous term.

§6. Legislative Initiative and Limits

(a) Councilors may introduce bills only within their assigned domains.

(b) A Councilor may co-sponsor legislation outside their domain only jointly with the Councilor responsible for that domain.

(c) No bill may have more than three total sponsors (one primary sponsor and up to two co-sponsors).

(d) Bills must be domain-relevant and administrative in nature, aimed at executing policy within the Council’s competencies, and shall not bundle unrelated matters.

§7. Enactment. Laws shall be adopted by a simple majority vote of the Council and require the assent of the Soberante. The Soberante may also enact laws by Royal Decree.

§8. The Soberante shall serve as Presidente del Consejo.

§9. The Alcalde Mayor shall hold one vote in the Council, as a Councilor.

§10. Terms. Members of the Royal Council serve terms aligned with the federal election cycle of the Imperial Federation. At the conclusion of each term, their service shall automatically end unless the Soberante renews their appointment for another term.

§11. Councilors shall follow Common Procedures.

---

## **ARTICLE VIII – THE STATE JUDICIARY (PODER JUDICIAL DEL ESTADO)**

§1. The State Judiciary shall consist of three Magistrates:

* The Soberante, serving as Magistrado Supremo.

* One Magistrado Consejero, chosen by the Royal Council from among its members, serving a term lasting one federal election cycle, and eligible for reappointment to successive terms.

* One Magistrado del Pueblo, elected by citizens of Tier II or Tier III, serving a term lasting one federal election cycle, and eligible for re-election to successive terms.

§2. Any Magistrate may preside over and decide individual cases in accordance with the laws of Florabís.

* A Magistrate may summon a jury of citizens when necessary.

* The full panel of three Magistrates shall convene only for exceptional matters of great importance to the State.

* Decisions of the full panel shall be issued by majority vote.

* The Magistrado Supremo shall hold the casting vote in the event of a tie.

§3. The State Judiciary shall:

* Interpret and apply this Constitution.

* Enforce the laws of Florabís.

* Resolve disputes among citizens.

* Hear criminal matters, farmland disputes, and mining-zone conflicts.

§4. The jurisdiction of the State Judiciary extends to the whole State of Florabís.

* The Magistrates shall serve collectively as the custodians of justice for the entire State.

* Any case involving the Constitution, laws, or institutions of the Imperial Federation shall be referred to the Federal Judiciary.

§5. Appeals may be heard by a different Magistrate or, in serious cases, by the full panel of Magistrates. The Magistrado Supremo may personally render judgment in exceptional cases.

---

## **ARTICLE IX – DEFENSE OF FLORABÍS (DEFENSA DE FLORABÍS)**

§1. The Florabís Defense Guard (Guardia de Defensa de Florabís) shall serve as the armed force of the State.

§2. The Soberante shall serve as Commander-in-Chief of the Guard, with authority to appoint officers and to direct military operations.

§3. The Guard shall:

* Protect San Alejandro de Guadalupe and all Florabís.

* Support the defense of the Imperial Federation when called.

* Secure public works, roads, and farmland.

* Maintain order in times of emergency.

§4. The Guard shall consist of two principal branches:

* Guardia Fronteriza (Border Guard): charged with defending the borders of Florabís, protecting farmland and infrastructure, and ensuring that all territory under the Florabís namelayer is properly reinforced and maintained.

* Guardia de Campaña (Field Guard): tasked with mobile defense operations, reinforcement of vulnerable areas, patrols of transport routes, rapid response to raids, and providing security for trading caravans, expeditions, and cooperative exercises with allied states.

§5. The Soberante may designate unsafe or unreinforceable areas as Tierras Restringidas (Restricted Lands), closed to settlement, farming, or construction unless expressly authorized.

§6. The Councilor for Defense and Security shall be a permanent seat on the Royal Council. This position shall be held by the Capitán de la Guardia, who shall serve as the chief officer responsible for the defense of Florabís and for advising the Soberante and the Council on matters of security.

§7. Mining shall occur only in zones approved by the Royal Council with the assent of the Soberante. Access shall be limited to citizens of Tier II or Tier III. Tier II shall access designated mining zones. Tier III shall enjoy access to all open mines of Florabís.

§8. The Capitán de la Guardia is the third in command of the State, under the authority of the Soberante and the Alcalde Mayor.

§9. The Capitán de la Guardia shall serve under the authority of the Soberante.

---

## **ARTICLE X – TERRITORIAL INTEGRITY**

§1. The territory of Florabís is under the authority of the Imperial Federation, and no part of it may be ceded, merged, or transferred except by lawful action of the Federal Government.

§2. As a condition of its Statehood, Florabís shall never cede, merge, or transfer land to the States of Söderrike, Regensburg, or Kraftsborg.

§3. Adjustments to internal subdivisions of Florabís shall be decreed by the Soberante, after review and recommendation by the Royal Council.

---

## **ARTICLE XI – AMENDMENTS AND RATIFICATION**

§1. Amendments may be proposed by the Soberante or by the Royal Council.

§2. Amendments shall require the assent of the Soberante and the approval of a majority of the Royal Council.

§3. This Constitution is hereby decreed, promulgated, and established as the supreme law of the State of Florabís, in union with the Imperial Federation, and as the internal Constitución del Estado Real de Florabís.

---

## **ARTICLE XII – FINAL PROVISION**

“With this Constitution, the Florabinos affirm our unity, our loyalty to the Imperial Federation, and their commitment to build a flourishing and welcoming home. Let this Constitution stand as the enduring foundation of our State.”
